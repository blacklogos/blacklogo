# Start Lesson 2.5: Competitive Intelligence & Analysis

Master competitive research and positioning - know your competitors better than they know themselves.

## Why Competitive Intelligence Matters

**Without it**: You're fighting blind
**With it**: You anticipate moves, find gaps, position strategically

## Part 1: Competitor Profiles

### Deep Dive on Main Competitors
```
Create comprehensive competitor profiles for:
1. Asana
2. Monday.com
3. ClickUp

For each competitor, research and document:

Company Overview:
- Founded when, by whom
- Company size and funding
- Market position and reputation
- Recent news and developments

Product Analysis:
- Core features and differentiators
- Pricing tiers and strategy
- Target customer segments
- Product roadmap signals

Marketing Strategy:
- Positioning and messaging
- Key marketing channels
- Content strategy and topics
- Ad campaigns (Google, LinkedIn)
- SEO keywords they rank for
- Social media presence and engagement

Customer Sentiment:
- Review analysis (G2, Capterra)
- Common complaints
- Praised features
- Switching reasons (to and from)

Strengths & Weaknesses:
- What they do better than us
- What we do better than them
- Market gaps they're not filling

Create detailed competitor profile documents.
```

```
- Enterprise: Custom (large orgs)

- Price sensitivity by segment: Sam (high), Maria (medium), Steve (high)

Recommendations:
```

## Part 7: Win/Loss Analysis

### Why Do We Win? Why Do We Lose?
```
Analyze 50 recent sales outcomes:

Wins (30 deals):
Reasons we won:
1. Faster setup (mentioned in 18 deals): "Asana took too long to configure"
2. AI prioritization (mentioned in 15 deals): "Unique feature, saves time"
3. Better for small teams (mentioned in 12 deals): "Not overwhelming"
4. Price value (mentioned in 8 deals): "More features for the price"
5. Better support (mentioned in 7 deals): "Responsive, helpful"

Losses (20 deals):
Reasons we lost:
1. Fewer integrations (mentioned in 12 deals): "Need Salesforce integration"
2. Less enterprise features (mentioned in 8 deals): "Need advanced permissions"
3. Brand recognition (mentioned in 6 deals): "Safer to go with Asana"
4. Existing tool migration (mentioned in 5 deals): "Too hard to switch"

Strategic implications:
- Double down: Setup speed, AI features, small team focus
- Address: Add top 10 integrations, build migration tools
- Position: "Modern Asana alternative for agile teams"

Create win/loss analysis report with competitive battle cards.
```

## Part 8: Competitive Battle Cards

```
Create battle card for competing against Asana:

Our advantages:
✅ 10-minute setup vs 2+ hours
✅ AI task prioritization (they don't have it)
✅ Better for small teams (less overwhelming)
✅ Comparable price with more value

⚠️ More integrations (100 vs our 50)
⚠️ Stronger brand recognition
⚠️ More advanced enterprise features

Key messages:
- "Asana is powerful but complex - we're powerful AND simple"
- "Our AI saves 30 min/day on prioritization"
- "You'll be up and running in 10 minutes, not 2 weeks"

Proof points:
- Customer quote: "We evaluated Asana but chose FocusFlow because..."
- Case study: Company X switched from Asana, saved 5 hours/week
- Demo: Show side-by-side setup comparison

Common objections and responses:
- "Asana has more integrations" → "We have the 50 most used. Which specific one do you need?"
- "Asana is more established" → "True, but they're built for enterprise. We're built for teams like yours."

Create complete battle cards for all 3 main competitors.
```
